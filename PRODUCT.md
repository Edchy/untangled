# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Curious, intelligent adults with no technical background. Possibly anxious about AI. They encounter Untangled via a link or recommendation, not a search for a course. They're reading alone, probably on a laptop or phone, in a relaxed moment.

## Product Purpose

A free, nonprofit, illustrated narrative that builds a genuine mental model of AI — starting from the first computers and ending with honest questions about what AI can and can't do. Success means a learner who finishes can explain how an LLM works to a friend, in their own words.

## Brand Personality

Unhurried, rigorous, warm. The emotional goal is the "click" feeling: something that was confusing finally makes sense, delivered with enough care that the reader trusts the explanation.

## Anti-references

Coursera, Duolingo, Medium, typical edtech — anything that feels like a course platform, uses progress bars as motivation, has corporate warmth, or leans on gamification. AI explainer content that is either too breezy ("it's just autocomplete!") or too technical.

## Design Principles

1. The design earns trust before the content does — first impressions must feel considered, not templated
2. Restraint is the point: one accent, generous whitespace, nothing decorative that isn't also meaningful
3. Show, don't tell — interactive moments clarify through doing, not through describing
4. Every slide has one job; the design enforces that, not just the writing
5. Warmth through craft, not through friendliness — no exclamation marks, no mascots, just careful work

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support. High contrast maintained in both light and dark modes.
