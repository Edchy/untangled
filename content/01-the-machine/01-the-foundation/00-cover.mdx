Before we can talk about AI, we need to talk about what AI runs on. And most things that seem complicated are just things nobody has taken apart for you yet.

This chapter takes apart a computer. You'll see how a single switch, on or off, nothing more, becomes the foundation for everything: letters, colours, photos, memory, software. How billions of those switches fit on a chip smaller than your thumbnail. And what's actually inside the machine you're using right now.

Once you see the foundation, everything built on top of it will start to make sense.

- Abstraction: how complexity hides in layers
- Binary: the language of switches
- Transistors: the switch inside everything
- Text and images: how meaning becomes bits
- Components: inside a modern computer
