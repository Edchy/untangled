To illustrate why all this stuff is of any use at all, let's imagine we have two numbers. Number A and number B. They could be anything. They could be 1 and 0. They could be 100 and 101. They could be 1,000,000 and 999,999. It doesn't matter. 
Now we want the machine to tell us if **A is bigger than B**.

Of course, we could just look at the numbers and figure it out ourselves in this case. But this is the most simple example of **a problem that a machine can solve**. And we want to understand how machines solve problems. If we can understand how to solve this problem then we can also understand that its just **a matter of scaling up** to solve more complex problems.
