## Logic gates answer tiny yes-or-no questions.

A logic gate is a small circuit with a simple rule. It looks at one or two inputs and produces one output.

An AND gate asks: are both inputs on? An OR gate asks: is at least one input on? These tiny questions can be joined together into larger systems.

This is how a computer begins to feel less like a black box. It is not thinking in the human sense. It is combining many simple rules very quickly.
