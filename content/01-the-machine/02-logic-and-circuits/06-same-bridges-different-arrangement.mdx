You're trying to reach a small town on the other side of a river.

One route crosses the river twice: two bridges, one after the other. **Both have to be passable** to get through. One washed-out bridge and you turn back. That's **AND**.

Another route splits into two roads, each with its own bridge. Take either one. **As long as one is standing,** you reach the town. You only turn back if both are gone. That's **OR**.

Same destination. Same bridges. **A different arrangement.**
