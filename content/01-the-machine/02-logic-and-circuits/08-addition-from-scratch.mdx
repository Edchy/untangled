Here's where it clicks.

Take an AND gate and one more gate: XOR. Think of XOR as "OR, but not if both are on."

Connect them to the same two inputs. What you get is a circuit that adds two binary numbers. The XOR gate gives you the sum. The AND gate catches the carry, the same way adding 9 + 1 gives you 10 and you carry the 1 into the next column.

Two gates. That's addition.

Chain millions of circuits like this and you have a processor. But at the bottom of that stack, it's still just gates. Which is still just switches.
