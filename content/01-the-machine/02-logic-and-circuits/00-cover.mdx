You've seen what a single switch does. But a computer can recognise your face, translate a sentence, generate an image. How do you get from one to the other?

The answer is gates. Connect two switches and they can answer questions together. There are only three kinds of gate, AND, OR, NOT, and every computation that has ever run on any computer is built from combinations of those three.

This chapter works through that step by step: how gates chain together, how they add numbers, how they remember things. By the end, you'll have seen, from scratch, how thinking machines think.

- AND, OR, NOT: the only three gates there are
- Chaining: how gates answer bigger questions
- Comparison: building logic from scratch
- Addition: arithmetic in binary
- Memory: how a circuit holds its state
