That comparison was tiny.
**Two inputs. Two gates. One output.**

But the important part is **the shape of the work**.
Start with a question the machine can answer with 1 or 0. Break it into smaller **yes/no questions**. Wire those questions together until the final wire means the thing you wanted to know.

That is what logic means here. Not a machine thinking like a person. Just **a rule, built out of tiny yes/no questions**.


Add more wires and more gates, and the same idea can compare bigger numbers. Add a different arrangement, and it can do **something else entirely**.
