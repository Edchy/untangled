## Early instructions were physical.

Before screens and keyboards, people stored instructions in things you could touch. A hole in a card might mean yes. No hole might mean no. A row of holes could describe a pattern.

This matters because it makes one idea feel less mysterious: information can be represented by marks.

If a machine can read the marks, the marks can guide the machine.

<Callout label="The aha">
A computer does not need to understand a card. It only needs to respond reliably to the pattern on it.
</Callout>
