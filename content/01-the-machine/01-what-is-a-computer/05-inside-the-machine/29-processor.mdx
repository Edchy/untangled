A processor is a chip packed with **billions of those transistors**, organized into circuits.
Those circuits can do simple things. Add two numbers. Compare two values. Move a bit from one place to another.
That is **all** a processor does.

But wire enough of those circuits together, coordinate them with a clock, and run them billions of times a second, and something remarkable happens.
You can run **any program ever written**.
