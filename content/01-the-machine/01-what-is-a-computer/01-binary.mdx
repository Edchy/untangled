## Binary is a very small alphabet.

Binary uses two symbols: 0 and 1. That can feel limiting until you remember that an alphabet with only two letters can still make very long messages.

In a computer, 0 and 1 are convenient because machines can reliably tell the difference between two states: off or on, low or high, no or yes.

The point is not that computers love the numbers zero and one. The point is that two clear states are easier to build with than many fuzzy ones.

<Callout>
Binary is not magic. It is a practical way to make information unambiguous for a machine.
</Callout>
