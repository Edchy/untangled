In 1945, the first electronic computer filled an entire room.
The switches inside were called vacuum tubes. Each one the size of a light bulb.
ENIAC had 18,000 of them.
It could calculate in seconds what took mathematicians days.

From a room. To a desk. To your pocket.
Same idea. Smaller machine.
