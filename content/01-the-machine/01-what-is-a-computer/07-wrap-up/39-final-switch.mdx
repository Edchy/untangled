You clicked this at the start of this chapter.

The light changed. 

A switch is not a computer.
But a computer is **nothing but switches**, arranged cleverly.

This is not a computer.
It’s just a switch.

But if you had enough of these…
and connected them the right way…

you’d have something that could **think**.

(last missing part lead in to logic gates, which are just arrangements of switches that can do simple math, and from that you can build a computer - few slides with interactive illustrations of logic gates, then a final slide with a simple computer built from logic gates, and how it can do math, and from that you can build a computer that can do anything)
