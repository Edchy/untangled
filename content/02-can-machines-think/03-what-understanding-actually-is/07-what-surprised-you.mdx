Most people are surprised in both directions.

Things that feel hard — writing a poem, explaining a concept, translating a sentence — current AI does reasonably well. Things that feel easy — tying a shoelace, catching a ball, understanding that a cup on a table might fall if the table is tilted — AI struggles with badly.

This pattern has a name: Moravec's paradox. The tasks that are hard for humans are often easy for machines. The tasks that are easy for humans — things we do without thinking — are often the hardest for machines to do at all.

Why? Because the "easy" things have been solved by millions of years of evolution. The hard things are recent additions to the human repertoire, and it turns out they're not as deep as they feel.
