The most important response to Searle is called the systems reply, and it goes like this:

Yes, the person in the room doesn't understand Chinese. But the *system* — the person plus the rulebook plus the room — does. Understanding isn't located in any single part. It's a property of the whole.

Searle's response: fine, imagine the person memorises the entire rulebook and leaves the room. Now they are the system. Do they understand Chinese?

The systems reply says: yes, at that point, arguably they do.

Searle says: that feels wrong.

And that's where it stops. Both sides are making reasonable arguments. Neither can prove their point definitively. This is not a failure of philosophy — it's the actual shape of the question.
