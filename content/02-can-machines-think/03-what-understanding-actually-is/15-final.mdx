You started this module with a question that felt simple: can machines think?

By now you know it isn't simple. You know why Turing sidestepped the definition. You know what the first AI programs could and couldn't do. You know why Searle's room still troubles people, and why the reply troubles them too.

You don't have an answer. Neither does anyone else. But you have something better than an answer: you know what makes the question hard, and you can hold it without needing to resolve it.

That's the right posture for everything that follows.

What follows is the story of what happened after Dartmouth — the years when the machines kept failing, the funding dried up, and the people working on AI had to ask whether the whole project had been a mistake.

It wasn't. But it came close.
