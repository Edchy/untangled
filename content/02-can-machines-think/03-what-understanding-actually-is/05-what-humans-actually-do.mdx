Here's something worth sitting with: we don't understand our own understanding.

When you recognise a face, you can't explain how. The process happens below the level of conscious access. When you understand a joke, you can't describe the steps your brain took to get from hearing the words to finding them funny. When you know a sentence is wrong before you can say why, something is operating that you have no visibility into.

We hold machines to a standard we can't meet ourselves.

That doesn't settle anything. But it does suggest that "understanding" is not as clear a concept as it feels when we're using it to draw a line between humans and machines.
