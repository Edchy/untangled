Remember the imitation game you played in the first chapter. You were watching for something. A slip. A response that didn't quite ring true.

You were using a sense you can't fully describe — a feeling for what a human response looks like versus what a machine response looks like.

Here's what's strange about that: the machines have gotten much better. The feeling has gotten less reliable.

Which raises the question we've been circling: if a machine can produce responses that are indistinguishable from a human's, is there still something missing? And if so, what is it?
