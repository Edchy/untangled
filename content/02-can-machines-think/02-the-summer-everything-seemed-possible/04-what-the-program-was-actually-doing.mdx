Let's be precise, because it matters.

The Logic Theorist didn't understand mathematics. It didn't know what a theorem meant. It had a toolbox of rules: "If your equation looks like this, you can change it to that."

Think of it like cracking a combination lock or brute-forcing a password. You don't need to understand the lock or what the password protects—you just try different combinations systematically until one works.

That's exactly what it did: tried rule combinations over and over, checking each result against the target theorem.

When it succeeded, it wasn't insight. It was persistence—searching through enough combinations until one fit.

Still impressive. Smart searching is genuinely hard. But it's not the same as mathematical understanding.
