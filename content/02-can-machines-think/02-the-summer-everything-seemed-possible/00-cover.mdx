The question is still open
Six years after Turing, ten men gathered for summer at a New Hampshire college. They called it the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence.

They had a proposal, a budget, and a conviction: every facet of intelligence could, in principle, be precisely described for machines to mimic.

They thought summer's end might see it done.

This is their story—not of failure, but of impressive beginnings and a gap that revealed how vast the problem truly was. The optimists were no fools; the mountain simply proved steeper.
