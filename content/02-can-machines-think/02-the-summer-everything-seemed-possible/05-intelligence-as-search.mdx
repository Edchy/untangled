This insight — that intelligence might be a form of search — became the core idea of early AI.

Newell and Simon followed the Logic Theorist with the General Problem Solver, a program designed to solve any problem that could be stated as a goal and a set of allowed moves. It worked by means-ends analysis: look at the current state, look at the goal, pick whatever move closes the gap the most.

It was elegant. If all problems can be turned into search problems, and if computers are very fast at searching, then maybe intelligence is just very fast search.

Try it yourself and see what you think.
