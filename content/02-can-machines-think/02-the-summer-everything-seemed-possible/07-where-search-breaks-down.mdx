Search works when the space of possibilities is manageable.

A simple puzzle has maybe a few thousand possible states. A computer can search all of them in milliseconds. No problem.

Chess has more possible games than there are atoms in the observable universe. Searching all of them is not an option, not even for the fastest computer imaginable.

Language is worse. The number of ways to continue a sentence is effectively infinite. Search alone is not enough.

The early optimists didn't fully appreciate this. They had solved the easy version of the problem and assumed the hard version was just more of the same.

It wasn't.
