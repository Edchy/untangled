The forbidden idea
In 1950, machine thinking wasn't just wrong—it was impossible. By definition.

Thinking belonged to humans. It required consciousness, a soul, the mysterious essence of personhood. Machines were tools—lifeless, soulless, incapable.

Turing turned to the skeptics: Prove it. Define thinking precisely. Show me exactly why machines can't do it.

No one could. And that was precisely his point.
