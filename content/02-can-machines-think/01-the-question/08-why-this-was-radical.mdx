In 1950, the idea that a machine might think was not just wrong — it was considered obviously, definitionally wrong.

Thinking was what humans did. It was tied to consciousness, to soul, to whatever made a person a person. Machines were tools. They didn't think. They couldn't.

Turing challenged the people who held this view: prove it. Tell me, precisely, what thinking is — and then show me the machine doesn't do it.

Nobody could. That was his point.
