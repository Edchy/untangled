And yet.

Turing had built machines — or designed them, or helped build them — and he was unsatisfied. Not because they were too slow or too limited. It was something else.

The machines could follow instructions. Billions of steps per second, each one perfectly. But they didn't seem to *know* what they were doing. They executed. They didn't understand.

Was that distinction real? Or was it just a feeling — a human reluctance to give the machine credit?

That was the question.
