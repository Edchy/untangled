Turing published his paper in 1950. He was prosecuted by the British government for being gay in 1952. He died in 1954, aged 41.

The question he asked outlived him by decades. It's still alive.

What happened next was that a group of people decided to stop asking whether machines could think — and start building ones that could. They thought it would take a summer.

It did not take a summer.
