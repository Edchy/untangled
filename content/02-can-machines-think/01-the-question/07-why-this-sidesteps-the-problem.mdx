Notice what Turing did not do.

He did not define thinking. He didn't try to open up a machine and look for whatever thinking is made of. He didn't ask whether the machine has feelings, or consciousness, or understanding.

He only asked: from the outside, can you tell the difference?

This is a deliberate move. Turing was saying: if you can't even tell a machine from a human in conversation, then whatever thinking is, the machine is doing enough of it to matter.

The question "can machines think?" gets replaced by something you can actually test.
