Alan Turing was a mathematician. But what set him apart wasn't the mathematics — it was a habit of taking impossible questions seriously.

Most people, confronted with a question like "can machines think?", would wave it away. Too vague. Too philosophical. Not the kind of thing you could get a grip on.

Turing did the opposite. He sat with the question until it started to have edges.
