Alan Turing was a mathematician, but his real gift was a peculiar discipline: he took impossible questions seriously.

Where others saw a question like “Can machines think?” and turned away, Turing stayed with it. Too vague, too philosophical, too elusive — most people would have left it there.
He did not. He held the question long enough for it to sharpen, until it began to have edges.
