The obvious move is to define "thinking" — to say exactly what it is, then check whether a machine does it.

Turing tried this. He found it didn't work.

Any definition you write down is either too broad (a thermostat "thinks" by that measure) or too narrow (it rules out things humans clearly do). The concept of thinking is not crisp enough to define cleanly.

So he did something clever. He threw the definition away.
