the switch:
The switch is the toggle. Flip it on, the room lights up — light mode. Flip it off, the room goes dark — dark mode. The user doesn't even realize they've set a preference, they were just playing.
And it layers meaning perfectly. The same gesture teaches abstraction, introduces binary (on/off, the most fundamental concept in computing), and controls the site's theme — all at once. Three things, one interaction, zero explanation needed.
4. The switch-as-theme-toggle idea

This is one of those ideas that sounds “cute” but is actually deeply right.

Why it works:

1. It reinforces learning

* ON / OFF → binary
* Physical → conceptual → system-level

2. It persists meaning

Every time they toggle dark/light later:

they’re repeating the first lesson

That’s rarely done well in products.
